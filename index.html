About Jennie Kim

Jennie Kim known mononymously as Jennie is a South Korean singer, rapper and global fashion icon best known as a member of the world-renowned K-pop girl group BLACKPINK. Born on January 16, 1996 in South Korea. Jennie spent several years studying in New Zealand before returning to Korea to pursue a career in music. She joined YG Entertainment as a trainee in 2010 and trained for nearly six years before debuting with BLACKPINK in August 2016. As the group’s main rapper and lead vocalist, Jennie quickly gained attention for her charismatic stage presence, distinctive voice and versatile performance style. In 2018, she became the first member of the group to debut as a solo artist with the single “SOLO,” which was both a commercial and critical success, topping charts and highlighting her individuality as an artist. Beyond music, Jennie is widely recognized for her influence in the fashion industry. Often referred to as the “Human Chanel,” she serves as a global ambassador for several luxury brands and is frequently seen at international fashion weeks. Her style—both elegant and edgy—has inspired millions of fans worldwide. Jennie’s impact extends far beyond the stage. With her talent, confidence, and trendsetting presence, she continues to shape global pop culture and remains one of the most influential figures in the entertainment industry today.
